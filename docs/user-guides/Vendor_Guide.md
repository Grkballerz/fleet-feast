## Getting Started

### Who Can Apply

Fleet Feast is for licensed food truck operators who:
- Have a valid business license
- Hold current health department permits
- Carry liability insurance
- Operate in the New York City area
- Want to expand into the catering market

### Application Process

**Step 1: Create Your Account**
1. Visit [fleetfeast.com](https://fleetfeast.com)
2. Click **Sign Up** and select **I'm a Vendor**
3. Enter your email and create a secure password
4. Verify your email address

**Step 2: Submit Your Application**
1. Log in and go to **Apply as Vendor**
2. Complete the multi-step application:

**Business Information**
- Business legal name
- DBA (Doing Business As) name
- Tax ID / EIN
- Business address
- Year established
- Truck description

**Required Documents**
Upload clear, current copies of:
- **Business License**: Must be valid and not expired
- **Health Department Permit**: Current health inspection certificate
- **Liability Insurance**: Minimum $1M coverage
- **Food Handler Certifications**: For all staff who handle food
- **Truck Photos**: Exterior shots showing your truck's appearance

**Menu and Pricing**
- Upload your full menu
- List cuisine type(s)
- Specify pricing model:
  - Per person packages
  - Per item pricing
  - Custom quotes
- Note dietary options (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free)

**Capacity and Availability**
- Minimum guest count you can serve
- Maximum guest count you can serve
- Service area (which NYC boroughs/areas)
- Event types you specialize in

**Step 3: Application Review**

After submission:
- Our team reviews your application within **3-5 business days**
- We verify all documents are current and valid
- You'll receive an email with the decision

**If Approved:**
- Your profile goes live immediately
- You can start accepting bookings
- Welcome email with next steps

**If Additional Information Needed:**
- We'll email you specifically what's missing
- Resubmit corrected/additional documents
- Review continues once received

**If Denied:**
- Email explains the reason
- Common reasons: expired permits, insufficient insurance, incomplete application
- You can reapply once issues are resolved

### Getting Your Profile Live

Once approved:
1. Complete your full profile (photos, menu, description)
2. Set your availability calendar
3. Configure your pricing
4. Your truck appears in search results immediately

## Managing Your Truck Profile

Your profile is your storefront. Make it compelling!

### Profile Sections

**Business Overview**
1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Profile**
2. Fill in or update:
   - **Truck Name**: What customers see in search
   - **Tagline**: One-sentence description (e.g., "Authentic Mexican street tacos")
   - **About**: Your story, specialties, what makes you unique (2-3 paragraphs)
   - **Cuisine Type(s)**: Select all that apply

**Photo Gallery**

High-quality photos dramatically increase bookings:

**Must-Have Photos:**
- Truck exterior (clean, well-lit)
- Food presentation (professional shots)
- Food preparation (shows cleanliness)
- Past events (shows your truck in action)

**Photo Tips:**
- Use natural lighting when possible
- Take photos from multiple angles
- Show food close-up and plated
- Update seasonally

**To Upload Photos:**
1. Go to **Profile** → **Gallery**
2. Click **Upload Photos**
3. Select up to 20 photos
4. Drag to reorder (first photo is your main image)
5. Click **Save Changes**

### Menu Management

**Creating Your Menu**

1. Go to **Profile** → **Menu**
2. Click **Add Menu Item**
3. For each item, provide:
   - Item name
   - Description
   - Price (if per-item pricing)
   - Dietary tags (vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, dairy-free)
   - Photo (optional but recommended)

**Menu Organization:**
- Group items by category (appetizers, mains, desserts)
- Use clear, appetizing descriptions
- Note any allergens
- Update for seasonal offerings

**Pricing Models**

Choose what works best for your business:

**Per Person Packages**
- Example: "$15 per person includes 2 tacos, rice, beans, drink"
- Good for: Standard catering, predictable service
- Set package tiers (basic, standard, premium)

**Per Item Pricing**
- Example: "Tacos $4 each, Burritos $8 each"
- Good for: A la carte service, customer flexibility
- List all items with individual prices

**Custom Quotes**
- Price varies by event specifics
- Good for: Large events, complex menus, customization
- You'll quote after receiving booking details

### Availability Calendar

Your calendar controls when you can receive booking requests:

**Setting Your Availability**

1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Calendar**
2. Select dates you're available for catering
3. Block dates when you're:
   - Already booked (automatically blocked when you accept a booking)
   - On vacation
   - Doing scheduled maintenance
   - Have other commitments

**Calendar Best Practices:**
- Keep calendar updated in real-time
- Block personal time off in advance
- Update immediately when accepting off-platform events (see [Platform Rules](#platform-rules-and-policies))
- Check weekly for accuracy

**Recurring Unavailability:**
- Block repeating dates (every Monday, first week of each month, etc.)
- Useful for regular maintenance or personal commitments

### Pricing Configuration

**Setting Your Rates**

1. Go to **Profile** → **Pricing**
2. Set your pricing structure:
   - Base rate (starting price)
   - Per person pricing
   - Minimum booking amount
   - Additional fees (travel, setup, etc.)

**Price Range Display:**
Customers see your price range ($, $$, $$$, $$$$):
- $ = Under $500
- $$ = $500-$1,000
- $$$ = $1,000-$2,000
- $$$$ = $2,000+

**Pricing Tips:**
- Research competitor pricing
- Factor in food costs, labor, travel, time
- Remember: Platform takes 15% commission (10% for loyalty bookings)
- Don't undervalue your service

### Service Area

Specify where you'll travel for events:

1. Go to **Profile** → **Service Area**
2. Select areas you serve:
   - Specific NYC boroughs
   - Nearby counties
   - Maximum travel distance from your base

**Travel Considerations:**
- Factor travel time into your schedule
- Consider charging travel fees for distant locations
- Be realistic about how far you'll go

## Handling Bookings

### Receiving Booking Requests

When a customer requests to book you:

1. You receive notification via:
   - Email
   - In-app notification (red badge on **Bookings**)
   - Dashboard alert

2. Click the notification or go to **Dashboard** → **Bookings** → **Requests**

3. Review the request details:
   - Event date and time
   - Location
   - Guest count
   - Event type
   - Special requests

### The 48-Hour Window

**Important:** You have **48 hours** to accept or decline each request.

**After 48 Hours:**
- Request automatically declines
- Customer is notified
- Impacts your acceptance rate metric
- Frequent timeouts may affect your visibility in search results

**Set Up Notifications:**
Enable mobile notifications or check email regularly to respond quickly.

### Accepting Bookings

**When to Accept:**
- Date and time work for your schedule
- Location is within your service area
- Guest count matches your capacity
- You can fulfill the menu/service requested

**How to Accept:**

1. Go to **Bookings** → **Requests**
2. Click on the booking request
3. Review all details carefully
4. Click **Accept Booking**
5. Optionally, add a message to the customer (introduce yourself, confirm details)
6. Customer receives notification and payment link
7. Once customer pays, booking is confirmed

**After Accepting:**
- Booking appears in **Upcoming Bookings**
- Date automatically blocks on your calendar
- Customer can message you in-app
- You receive payment 7 days after the event (pending no disputes)

### Declining Bookings

**Valid Reasons to Decline:**
- Already booked (calendar wasn't updated)
- Outside service area
- Guest count doesn't match your capacity
- Can't fulfill special requests
- Weather or safety concerns

**How to Decline:**

1. Go to **Bookings** → **Requests**
2. Click on the booking request
3. Click **Decline Booking**
4. **Select a reason** (helps customers understand):
   - Already booked on that date
   - Outside my service area
   - Guest count too large/small
   - Cannot accommodate special requests
   - Other (explain)
5. Optionally, suggest alternatives:
   - Different date
   - Modified menu
   - Refer another truck
6. Click **Confirm Decline**

**Decline Carefully:**
- High decline rates may hurt your search ranking
- Customers can see your acceptance rate
- Only decline when genuinely necessary

### Communicating with Customers

All customer communication happens in-app:

**Accessing Messages**

1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Messages**
2. Click on a booking conversation
3. Type and send messages

**What to Discuss:**
- Menu customization and preferences
- Dietary restrictions and allergies
- Setup logistics (parking, power, timing)
- Event timeline
- Payment and pricing questions
- Weather contingency plans

**Communication Best Practices:**
- Respond within 24 hours (faster is better)
- Be professional and friendly
- Confirm all important details in writing
- Set clear expectations
- Update customers if issues arise

**CRITICAL RULE:**
- **NEVER share or request phone numbers, emails, or social media**
- All contact info sharing is prohibited
- Violations result in warnings, suspension, or ban
- See [Anti-Circumvention Policy](#anti-circumvention-policy)

### Day-of-Event Coordination

**Before the Event:**
- Review booking details 24 hours prior
- Check weather forecast
- Prepare and pack food
- Fuel up your truck
- Confirm you have customer's in-app contact

**Arrival:**
- Arrive 30-60 minutes before service time for setup
- Park in designated area
- Set up equipment, signage, serving area
- Message customer when ready

**During Service:**
- Provide friendly, professional service
- Maintain food safety standards
- Keep serving area clean
- Monitor food supply and adjust as needed

**Wrap-Up:**
- Clean and pack your equipment
- Leave the area clean
- Thank the customer
- Confirm event completion in your dashboard

**If Issues Arise:**
- Communicate immediately with the customer
- Document problems (photos if relevant)
- Message Fleet Feast support if needed
- Never abandon an event without communication

## Payments and Earnings

### How Escrow Works

Fleet Feast uses escrow to protect both you and customers:

**Payment Flow:**
1. Customer pays when they confirm the booking
2. Platform holds payment securely (escrow)
3. You fulfill the event
4. 7-day dispute window begins after event date
5. If no disputes: Payment releases to you automatically
6. If dispute filed: Payment held until resolution

**Why 7 Days?**
- Gives customers time to report issues
- Protects you from chargebacks
- Ensures fair resolution of problems

### Commission Structure

**Standard Commission: 15%**

Example calculation:
- Customer pays: $1,000
- Platform commission (15%): $150
- Your payout: $850

**Loyalty Commission: 10%**

When a customer books you repeatedly:
- Their 2nd+ booking receives 5% discount
- Platform reduces commission to 10%
- You earn the same, customer saves money
- Encourages repeat business

Example:
- Customer's 2nd booking quote: $1,000
- Customer pays: $950 (5% loyalty discount)
- Platform commission (10%): $95
- Your payout: $855

**Important:**
- Commission is deducted before payout
- You receive payout amount, not the full booking price
- Factor commission into your pricing

### Payout Schedule

**Automatic Payouts:**
- Occur 7 days after each event
- Deposited to your linked bank account
- No action needed from you

**Payout Timeline:**
- Event completes: Day 0
- 7-day dispute window: Days 1-7
- Payout processes: Day 8
- Funds in your account: Days 9-11 (depending on your bank)

**Example:**
- Event on Monday, December 1
- Payout processes Monday, December 8
- Funds arrive Tuesday-Thursday, December 9-11

### Viewing Your Earnings

**Earnings Dashboard:**

1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Payouts**
2. View:
   - **Pending**: Upcoming payouts (in dispute window)
   - **Completed**: Past payouts
   - **Total Earnings**: All-time revenue

**Payout Details:**
- Booking ID and event date
- Customer name
- Booking amount
- Commission deducted
- Net payout
- Payout date
- Status (pending, processing, completed)

**Download Records:**
- Export payout history as CSV
- Use for bookkeeping and taxes
- Filter by date range

### Taxes and Reporting

**Your Responsibility:**
- Fleet Feast issues 1099-K forms if you earn $600+ per year
- Track your income and expenses
- Pay applicable self-employment and income taxes
- Consult a tax professional for guidance

**Available Records:**
- Detailed payout history
- Per-event breakdowns
- Annual earning summaries

### Handling Refunds and Disputes

**Customer Cancellations:**

If a customer cancels before the event:
- **7+ days before**: They get 100% refund, you receive nothing
- **3-6 days before**: They get 50% refund, you receive 50% (minus commission)
- **< 3 days before**: They get no refund, you receive full payout (minus commission)

**Disputes:**

If a customer files a dispute:
- Payment is held until resolution
- You'll be notified via email and dashboard
- You have 48 hours to respond with your side
- Admin reviews and makes decision

**Common Disputes:**
- Food quality complaints
- Late arrival
- Wrong/missing menu items
- Quantity issues

**Protecting Yourself:**
- Document your service (photos of setup, food)
- Save all in-app messages
- Arrive on time and communicate delays
- Fulfill exactly what was agreed upon

## Reviews and Ratings

### How Reviews Work

After each event, customers can leave:
- **Star Rating**: 1-5 stars
- **Written Review**: Their experience description

Reviews are:
- Tied to verified bookings only (no fake reviews)
- Visible on your profile
- Factored into your average rating
- Permanent (but you can respond)

### Maintaining Your Rating

**Your Overall Rating:**
- Average of all customer ratings
- Displayed prominently on your profile
- Impacts search ranking
- Influences booking decisions

**Rating Benchmarks:**
- 4.8-5.0: Excellent
- 4.5-4.7: Very Good
- 4.0-4.4: Good
- Below 4.0: Needs Improvement

### Responding to Reviews

**How to Respond:**

1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Reviews**
2. Find the review
3. Click **Respond**
4. Write your response
5. Click **Post Response**

**Response Best Practices:**

**For Positive Reviews:**
- Thank the customer sincerely
- Mention specific aspects they appreciated
- Invite them to book again
- Keep it brief and genuine

Example:
> "Thank you so much for the kind words! We're thrilled the tacos were a hit at your event. Hope to serve you again soon!"

**For Negative Reviews:**
- Stay calm and professional
- Acknowledge their concerns
- Explain what happened (if applicable)
- Offer to make it right
- Take the high road

Example:
> "We apologize for the delay in arrival. Traffic was unexpectedly heavy that day. We should have communicated better. We've implemented new protocols to prevent this in the future. Please reach out so we can make this right."

**Don'ts:**
- Don't argue or get defensive
- Don't insult the customer
- Don't blame the customer
- Don't make excuses
- Don't reveal private information

### Handling Negative Feedback

**If You Receive a Low Rating:**

1. Read the review carefully and objectively
2. Identify if there's legitimate feedback
3. Respond professionally
4. Implement improvements if needed
5. Focus on providing excellent service going forward

**Improving Your Rating:**
- Consistent great service increases good reviews
- One bad review won't tank your rating
- Focus on what you can control
- Learn from constructive criticism

**Disputing False Reviews:**
- If a review is fake or violates policies, contact support
- Provide evidence why it should be removed
- Fleet Feast investigates and makes final decision

## Platform Rules and Policies

### Anti-Circumvention Policy

**The Rule:**
All transactions MUST happen on the Fleet Feast platform.

**Prohibited Activities:**
- Sharing phone numbers with customers
- Sharing email addresses
- Sharing social media handles
- Attempting to book customers directly off-platform
- Requesting payment outside the platform
- Any attempt to bypass the platform fee

**Why This Matters:**
- Protects both you and customers
- Ensures escrow protection
- Maintains insurance coverage
- Keeps records for dispute resolution
- Platform sustainability

**Detection:**
Our system automatically scans messages for:
- Phone numbers in any format
- Email addresses
- Social media handles
- URLs to external sites
- Coded language ("call me", "my number", etc.)

**Penalties:**
- 1st offense: Warning
- 2nd offense: 30-day suspension
- 3rd offense: Permanent ban

**Don't Risk It:**
- In-app messaging is sufficient for coordination
- Customers can't share contact info either
- You'll lose all future bookings if banned

### Cancellation Expectations

**You Should Only Cancel If:**
- Emergency (illness, truck breakdown)
- Safety concerns (severe weather, unsafe event conditions)
- Absolutely unavoidable circumstances

**Never Cancel Because:**
- You got a better offer elsewhere
- You don't feel like working
- The booking is "too small"
- You want to book someone off-platform

**Cancellation Penalties:**

| Timing | Penalty | Customer Impact |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| 7+ days before | No penalty | Customer offered replacement or refund |
| 3-6 days before | $100 penalty | Customer offered replacement or refund |
| < 3 days before | $200 penalty | Customer offered replacement, refund, + $100 credit |
| No-show | $500 penalty | Customer gets full refund + $100 credit |

**How to Cancel:**

1. Go to **Bookings** → **Upcoming**
2. Click the booking
3. Click **Cancel Booking**
4. Select reason (required)
5. Explain circumstances in detail
6. Confirm cancellation

**After Cancellation:**
- Customer is notified immediately
- Fleet Feast attempts to find replacement vendor
- Penalty is deducted from your next payout
- Incident is recorded on your account

**Excessive Cancellations:**
- Pattern of cancellations may lead to suspension
- Your reliability affects platform reputation
- Only accept bookings you can fulfill

### Penalty System

Fleet Feast tracks violations to maintain quality:

**Violation Types:**
- Contact info sharing attempts
- Late arrivals (60+ minutes)
- Cancellations within 3 days
- No-shows
- Policy violations
- Poor customer service

**Progressive Penalties:**
- **1st offense**: Warning
  - Notification sent
  - Violation logged
  - No immediate penalty

- **2nd offense**: 30-Day Suspension
  - Account temporarily deactivated
  - Cannot receive new bookings
  - Must fulfill existing commitments
  - Reactivation after 30 days (if approved)

- **3rd offense**: Permanent Ban
  - Account permanently deactivated
  - No reactivation
  - Outstanding payouts released (minus penalties)

### Appeals Process

If you believe a penalty was unfair:

1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Violations**
2. Find the violation
3. Click **Appeal**
4. Provide detailed explanation and evidence:
   - What happened from your perspective
   - Why the penalty is incorrect
   - Any supporting documentation (photos, receipts, etc.)
5. Submit appeal

**Review Process:**
- Appeals reviewed within 5 business days
- Admin examines all evidence
- Decision is final
- You're notified of outcome

**Appeal Tips:**
- Provide factual, specific information
- Include timestamps and details
- Attach relevant screenshots or photos
- Stay professional in your explanation

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### I'm not receiving booking requests

**Possible Reasons:**
- Calendar has limited availability
- Profile is incomplete
- Pricing is too high/low
- Poor ratings
- Service area is too limited

**Solutions:**
- Update your availability calendar
- Complete all profile sections
- Review your pricing
- Improve service to boost ratings
- Expand service area if feasible

#### I missed the 48-hour acceptance window

**What Happens:**
- Request auto-declines
- Customer is notified
- Affects your acceptance rate

**Prevention:**
- Enable email notifications
- Check dashboard daily
- Set up mobile alerts
- Respond as soon as you see requests

#### A customer is trying to share contact info

**What to Do:**
- Do NOT respond with your contact info
- Politely remind them all communication must stay in-app
- Report the incident to Fleet Feast support
- Continue using in-app messaging only

**Example Response:**
> "To protect both of us, all communication must stay in the Fleet Feast app per the platform policies. I'm happy to answer any questions here!"

#### Customer is unhappy with my service

**Immediate Actions:**
- Apologize sincerely
- Try to resolve the issue on-site if possible
- Offer to make it right
- Document what happened

**After the Event:**
- Message them in-app to apologize and offer resolution
- Prepare for potential dispute
- Gather evidence (photos, timeline, receipts)
- Respond professionally if they file a dispute

#### I need to cancel an upcoming booking

**Steps:**
1. Assess if cancellation is truly necessary
2. Check how many days until the event
3. Go to **Bookings** → **Upcoming**
4. Click the booking
5. Click **Cancel Booking**
6. Select reason and explain thoroughly
7. Understand penalty will apply
8. Confirm cancellation

**Then:**
- Message customer to apologize personally
- Suggest alternative trucks if you can
- Accept the penalty
- Learn from the situation

#### My payout is less than expected

**Check:**
1. Go to **Payouts** → payout in question
2. Review breakdown:
   - Booking amount
   - Commission (15% or 10%)
   - Any penalties deducted
   - Net payout

**Common Reasons for Less:**
- Commission was deducted (standard)
- Customer used loyalty discount (commission is 10%)
- Penalty from cancellation or violation
- Partial refund issued due to dispute

**If Still Incorrect:**
- Click **Report Payout Issue**
- Provide expected amount and calculation
- Support will investigate

#### I received a violation warning

**Steps:**
1. Go to **Dashboard** → **Violations**
2. Read the violation details
3. Understand what happened
4. If it's accurate:
   - Take responsibility
   - Ensure it doesn't happen again
   - Improve your practices
5. If it's inaccurate:
   - File an appeal
   - Provide evidence
   - Wait for review

**Prevention:**
- Read and follow all policies
- Never share contact info
- Fulfill bookings as agreed
- Communicate delays/issues
- Maintain professionalism
